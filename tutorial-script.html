QC Vendor Portal — Tutorial Script

Glance-friendly script for screen recording. 14 clips · ~7 min total.
SHOW — what to have on screen SAY — narration ADMIN — admin-only feature
1. Sign in
~30s
SHOW
/login
SAY
To get into the portal, go to qc-vendor-portal.vercel.app. Type your email, hit Send sign-in link, and check your inbox. Click the link in the email and you're in. The link expires in 15 minutes and only works once — if it expires, just request a new one.
2. The big picture
~45s
SHOW
/queue — point at top nav
SAY
The nav across the top follows the workflow. Queue is where orders land. Merge handles duplicates. Batches is what's been shipped. Billing and Payments handle the money side. Ledger is your running balance. Out of Stock and Returns are operational tabs you'll touch occasionally. Once a day you'll probably hit Queue and Batches. Everything else only when there's something to do.
3. Pulling orders
~45s
SHOW
Queue → Pull new orders → Google Sheet → Pull
SAY
Hit Pull new orders. The Google Sheet is already configured, so just click Pull. New orders show up in the queue with red URGENT bars on the left if they're flagged. Duplicate orders sharing an email get a yellow Merge tag. Click Verify against ShipStation up top and the portal confirms each one exists in SS.
4. Per-order notes + stock status
~45s
SHOW
Click an order # — modal opens
SAY
Click any order number or the pencil icon to open notes. Top section is whole-order notes — handy for things like "bad address". Bottom section is per-item — if a SKU is out of stock or backordered, flag it here. The status carries to every other order that has the same SKU automatically, and to future orders that come in.
5. Merging duplicates
~45s
SHOW
Merge tab → click Merge on a group
SAY
When two orders share the same email and address, merge them so the customer gets one shipment. Pick the order number you want to keep, click Merge, done. The portal moves all items onto the surviving order. After this, go to ShipStation and finish the merge in their UI too — the portal flags both shipments with a Merge tag to make them easy to find.
6. Fetching shipped labels into a batch
~60s
SHOW
Batches → Fetch Printed Labels → batch detail
SAY
After you've printed labels in ShipStation, come back here and hit Fetch Printed Labels. Pick the date range — it defaults to since the last fetch. The portal pulls every shipment that has a label, creates a batch, and matches up the items. If something was on the order but not on the label, that item stays in the queue as a partial. Open the batch to see what's in it.
7. Creating an invoice from a batch
~45s
SHOW
Batch detail → review lines → Create Invoice
SAY
Each line shows the cost of goods and the per-item handling charge. Adjust any prices if needed — changes save back to the SKU catalog. When the totals look right, hit Create Invoice. It lands on the Billing tab and the ledger updates.
8. Reconciling when SS items differ
~30s
SHOW
Queue → click refresh icon on an order
SAY
If you swapped an item in ShipStation — say, an out-of-stock substitution — click the refresh icon on the queue row. The portal pulls the current ShipStation items and shows a diff: add, remove, qty change. Click Apply and the portal matches what was actually shipped.
9. Payments — send + receive
~60s
SHOW
Billing → Send Payment → fill dialog
SAY
I send a payment by clicking Send Payment, entering the amount, and allocating it across one or more open invoices. Anything left over rides as account credit. On the vendor side, you'll see it on the Payments tab marked "pending receipt". Click Mark received when the money lands. That's when it counts on the ledger and invoices flip to paid.
10. Manual invoice
~30s
SHOW
Billing → + Manual invoice
SAY
For one-off charges or carry-over balances, hit Manual invoice. Quick mode is just a description and an amount. Itemized mode lets you build a full line-by-line invoice. Either way it lands on the ledger the same as any other invoice.
11. Out of stock screen
~30s
SHOW
Out of Stock tab
SAY
Every SKU you've flagged as out-of-stock, backordered, or discontinued shows up here grouped by SKU. When stock's back, hit Mark in-stock and the flag clears across every order using that SKU. The row stays so you can push the update to the storefront — that one-click sync is coming soon.
12. Returns
~30s
SHOW
Returns tab → Log return → Mark received
SAY
When a customer returns, log it here with the order number and reason. The return shows as pending until the vendor receives the items — they hit Mark received, enter the credit amount, and that posts a credit against the outstanding balance.
13. Managing users
~20s
SHOW
Settings → Users & access
SAY
Only emails on this list can sign in. Add anyone who needs access — vendor, helpers, whoever. Remove them to instantly revoke.
14. Billing rules + SKUs
~30s
SHOW
Settings → Billing rules, then SKUs
SAY
Billing rules sets the handling charge — by default any item under $40 carries 50 cents. Change it here, applies to future invoices. SKUs is your catalog — costs auto-populate from the Google Sheet on first sight, but you can edit any of them.
